Workflow: Registration Page for an Assembly

Step details

Agree target categories

Decide which demographic categories will be used to stratify the selection — for example gender, age bracket, ethnicity, region, etc. These categories drive the form fields on the registration page, so they need to be agreed before the page can be built.

Agree non-target info to collect

We need to collect other information. The main kinds are:

  • Contact info — name, email, phone, address
  • Eligibility — ask them to confirm they are eligible
  • Consent — ask them to consent to us holding their data
Agree full URL & short-URL

Decide both URLs up front: the full URL the page will live at, and the short-URL that will redirect to it. Both are needed before invite materials can be drafted, because the short-URL is printed on the invite and the QR code is generated from it.

Generate QR code

The QR code is generated from the short-URL (not the full URL — the short-URL keeps the QR simpler and matches what's printed alongside it on the invite).

Invite materials — draft, review, print, post
  1. Draft — using the agreed full URL, short-URL and QR code.
  2. Review by team and client.
  3. Send to printer once agreed — at least a week before printing and going into the post.
  4. Printer prints and posts the invites.
Develop the registration page & related bits — what's on it

The registration page is built up from these pieces:

  • Full URL and short-URL redirect set up
  • Logo
  • Intro text
  • The form itself
  • Auto-reply email template
  • Thank-you page
  • Link to the page added to the "all open registrations" page

Then reviewed by team and client.

Team testing — what gets checked

Team members fill out the form (sometimes more than once) and verify:

  • The page itself looks correct
  • The intro text is accurate
  • The form options are correctly recorded in the system when submitted
  • The "thank you for registering" page looks good
  • The auto-reply email is sent correctly and looks good
Registration goes live

The page goes live once invites are posted and is typically open for around 3 weeks.

People who receive an invite have three ways to register:

  1. Scan the QR code on the paper invite and fill in the form.
  2. Type the short-URL from the paper invite and fill in the form.
  3. Phone the number on the invite — the person answering uses the "all open registrations" page to find the right registration link, then fills in the form on the caller's behalf.
Registration closes & selection happens

At the end of the live window the registration form is taken offline, and the stratified selection can run against the registrants.

Glossary

Full URL
The URL the registration page actually lives at. Examples:
  • https://opendlp.sortitionlab.org/r/cambridge-climate-assembly
  • https://democraticlottery.org/r/cambridge-climate-assembly  (could even be on a separate domain)
Short-URL
A short URL that redirects to the full URL. It's printed on paper invites for people to type in, and the QR code is generated from it. Examples:
  • https://demlot.org/r/cbgclimate
  • https://demlot.org/cca
"All open registrations" page
A page listing every currently-open registration. Often also lists recently-closed ones, so a viewer can see what was open in the recent past.
  • Current UK version: https://www.sortitionfoundation.org/register_which
  • Used by phone staff: when an invitee phones the number on the invite, the person answering opens this page to find the registration link to use.
  • Currently public, but not linked from anywhere.
